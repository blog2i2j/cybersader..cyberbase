**AI:**

If you're looking for outdoor adventure, cliff jumping, hiking, and budget-friendly spots in Texas while avoiding big cities, here are some great options that are a reasonable drive from Indiana:

### **1\. Garner State Park (Hill Country)**

- **Why?** Crystal-clear Frio River, hiking trails, and stunning limestone bluffs.
- **Activities:** Hiking, tubing, swimming, and even some spots for cliff jumping into the river.
- **Cost:** Cheap camping options and park entrance fees.
- **Bonus:** It’s peaceful and away from big city crowds.

### **2\. Devils River (West Texas)**

- **Why?** One of the most remote and pristine rivers in Texas.
- **Activities:** Kayaking, fishing, swimming, and cliff jumping in certain spots.
- **Cost:** Camping is affordable, but access is limited, so plan ahead.
- **Bonus:** Limited access means fewer people and untouched nature.

### **3\. Palo Duro Canyon (Panhandle)**

- **Why?** Known as the "Grand Canyon of Texas" with stunning red rock formations.
- **Activities:** Epic hiking, mountain biking, and camping.
- **Cost:** Low park fees and camping rates.
- **Bonus:** Amazing stargazing at night due to low light pollution.

### **4\. Pedernales Falls State Park (Hill Country)**

- **Why?** Beautiful waterfalls and a rugged landscape perfect for adventure.
- **Activities:** Hiking, swimming, bouldering, and exploring waterfalls.
- **Cost:** Cheap entry fee and camping options.
- **Bonus:** Close to other great spots like Hamilton Pool (a beautiful natural grotto).

### **5\. Jacob’s Well (Hill Country)**

- **Why?** A natural underwater cave system with a deep, clear swimming hole.
- **Activities:** Swimming and cliff jumping.
- **Cost:** Free or small reservation fee.
- **Bonus:** One of the clearest and most unique swimming holes in Texas.

### **6\. Caprock Canyons State Park (Panhandle)**

- **Why?** Less crowded than Palo Duro but with amazing red rock canyons.
- **Activities:** Hiking, backpacking, and spotting free-roaming bison.
- **Cost:** Low entrance and camping fees.
- **Bonus:** One of the best off-the-grid spots in Texas.

### **7\. Blue Hole Regional Park (Wimberley)**

- **Why?** A deep blue natural spring-fed pool with rope swings.
- **Activities:** Swimming, hiking, and relaxing in the cypress trees.
- **Cost:** Small entrance fee, camping nearby.
- **Bonus:** The water stays cool year-round!

### **8\. Colorado Bend State Park (Hill Country)**

- **Why?** A mix of rugged wilderness, waterfalls, and caves.
- **Activities:** Gorman Falls hike, cave exploring, swimming in the river.
- **Cost:** Low-cost camping and park fees.
- **Bonus:** Remote feel but still accessible.

### **9\. Inks Lake State Park (Central Texas)**

- **Why?** A great mix of adventure and relaxation.
- **Activities:** Cliff jumping at Devil’s Waterhole, hiking, and kayaking.
- **Cost:** Affordable camping, day-use fees.
- **Bonus:** One of the best cliff-jumping spots in Texas.

### **10\. Lake Whitney (Near Waco)**

- **Why?** Great for water sports, camping, and adventure.
- **Activities:** Cliff jumping, hiking, and fishing.
- **Cost:** Budget-friendly with campsites.
- **Bonus:** Some of the best hidden swimming spots in Texas.

### **Which One Should You Choose?**

- **For the most epic cliff jumping:** Jacob’s Well, Devil’s Waterhole, Lake Whitney.
- **For hiking and canyons:** Palo Duro, Caprock Canyons.
- **For rivers and waterfalls:** Garner State Park, Pedernales Falls, Colorado Bend.
- **For a remote adventure:** Devils River.

Most of these spots are **remote, budget-friendly, and away from big cities** while offering plenty of outdoor activities. You could plan a road trip hitting multiple parks in Hill Country or mix in some West Texas destinations for a rugged adventure.
